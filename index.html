Miss Universe

The Miss Universe competition was established in 1952 as a means to celebrate women, diversity, and "to provide the tools which help women to be their personal best." Using the Miss Universe Organisation's official list, we tallied up all the winners and found out which five countries have produced the most Miss Universe titleholders. The United States, Venezuela, and Puerto Rico (respectively) are the top three countries with the most winners. Sweden and the Philippines are tied for fourth place, producing three winners each throughout the years.

About


Since 1952, the Miss Universe competition has been a global event, providing a platform for women around the world "to be their personal best." First televised in 1955, the annual pageant airs worldwide and raked in about 5.21 million viewers in the US alone in 2017. All of the competitors, of course, vie for the crown - but certain countries have produced more winners than others throughout the history of the competition. We rounded up every Miss Universe and, using the Miss Universe Organisation's official list, figured out which five countries have borne the most titleholders since 1952. Keep scrolling to see which countries have taken home the most crowns.
